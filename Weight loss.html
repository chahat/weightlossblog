Stop Filling Your Body With Soda… And Added Sugar, for That Matter

Added sugars — sweeteners added to foods or beverages when they are processed or prepared — are little more than empty calories that can lead to weight gain and even obesity, which increases the risk for chronic diseases like diabetes, heart disease and cancer. Soda is one of the leading contributors of added sugars in the diet, along with cakes, pies, sweetened dairy products (think: ice cream) and even breakfast bars and cereal. 

Water Is Your Secret Weapon — We Mean It

Hydration is important for everyone, but it can also be a key component of your weight-loss efforts. In addition to keeping your body’s engine burning, water helps to stave off hunger: The more you drink, the less room you have for consuming calories. You should utilize every sip of this refreshing weight-loss tool.
